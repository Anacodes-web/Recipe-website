Party-Jollof

Nigeria Party Jollof: A flavorful, spicy, and iconic one-pot dish that's a staple at Nigerian parties and gatherings, made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and spices.

  • preptime: 10mins
  • cooktime: 53mins
  • Total Time: 63mins
  • servings: 6 or more

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 cups rice (washed)
  • 3 fresh tomatoes
  • 6 large chili red bell peppers (tatashe)
  • 4 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo)
  • 5 onion bulbs
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1-inch root ginger
  • 4 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1/ 2 teaspoon thyme
  • 1/ 4 tablespoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon crayfish powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground chili pepper
  • 4 seasoning cubes
  • 5 bay leaves
  • 4 cups vegetable stock or water
  • 1/ 2 cup vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

PROCEDURE

    Blend the fresh tomatoes, chili bell peppers (tatashe), scotch bonnet peppers, garlic, ginger and four onion bulbs in a blender till a smooth purée is formed. WINNIE'S NOTE
  1. Chop the tomatoes and peppers into tiny bits before placing in the blender and add little water to make the blending process easy.
  2. Afterwards, add the vegetable oil in a large pot and allow to heat up on medium heat.
  3. Chop the remaining onion bulb and transfer to the hot oil. Sauté for 45 seconds until translucent.
  4. Next add the tomato paste and leave to fry for about 5-7 minutes or until very crumbly. Stir occasionally.
  5. Then transfer the blended tomato purée and leave to fry for another 5-10 minutes or until completely dry. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.
  6. Add the vegetable stock (or water) to the pot along with the curry powder, thyme, seasoning cubes, ground pepper, nutmeg and salt to taste. Stir to combine.
  7. Afterwards, add the washed rice, stir to combine and slip in the bay leaves.
  8. Cut out the foil paper and cover the rice before putting the lid on the pot.

    9. NOTE: It is not water that makes rice soft, it is steam. So, using foil paper helps trap the steam inside the pot and this will make the rice soft faster and will prevent it from getting soggy and meshed up and you won't need to add any more water.

  9. To allow for perfect cooking, make sure your heat is reduced to the lowest and then cook for 35 minutes or until the rice is perfectly cooked.
  10. When the jollof rice is cooked, take out the bay leaves and discaro, then mix to combine. Take off the heat and allow it to cool down by covering the pot halfway with the lid.

    11. NOTE: Vegetable stock or water measurement and cooking time may vary depending on the type of rice you are using. Follow packet instructions for rice to water ratio and cooking time but the cooking method above remains same for all types of rice.
